Runescape

A History of the Most Popular Free-to-Play Game


Runescape Development Timeline:

  • 1998 - DeviousMUD, the forerunner to Runescape, was developed by Andrew, Paul, and Ian Gower.
  • 1999 - DeviousMUD was scrapped, then rewritten and released as an open beta for a week, before being withdrawn.
  • 2001 - The game was once again rewritten and given a graphical adjustment, before being released as an open beta under the name of Runescape.
  • 2001 - The Gower brothers and Constant Tedder create Jagex to better handle the business aspects of the game.
  • 2002 - A membership service is added, granting a variety of bonus game features and content for a monthly fee.
  • 2003 - A completely rewritten and graphically reworked version of the game, under the name of Runescape 2, is released in closed beta.
  • 2004 - Runescape 2 is renamed Runescape and released as the live game, while the old game is renamed Runescape Classic and made available as a separate game.
  • 2006 - Runescape Classic is closed to new accounts due to cheating. Meanwhile Runescape receives a game engine improvement.
  • 2007 - Due to widespread issues with cheating, Jagex cracks down and makes several major changes to the game, including removing the player-versus-player (PvP) from the Wilderness.
  • 2008 - Runescape is given a major graphical overhaul, known as Runescape High Detail.
  • 2009 - Mark Gerhard is appointed as CEO of Jagex.
  • 2011 - After a player poll where the overwhelming number of players voted to bring back PvP to the Wilderness, Jagex rolls back several of their major changes from 2007.
  • 2012 - A complete rework of the combat system, known as the Evolution of Combat is released as a closed beta, then later released to the live game.
  • 2013 - An old complete game save from 2007 is discovered, and after a brief poll of player interest, is released as a new game, known as Old School Runescape.
  • 2013 - Runescape 3 is released as Runescape, adding a reworked soundtrack, new user features, and a focus on player influenced content.
  • 2014 - Prifddinas, the single largest city in the game and a central hub to plenty of high-level content, is released alongside a quest.
  • 2016 - Runescape's first Elite Skill, Invention, is released.
